🔍 On the job market! I'm seeking research positions in HCI, Responsible AI, and AI evaluation & auditing, in both academia and industry. Get in touch.

I build evaluation infrastructure that puts communities in control of AI: methods and tools that let the people affected by AI systems define, measure, and audit what counts as good performance.

  • Infrastructure & Behavior · quantitative work using causal inference to understand how infrastructures such as broadband and LLMs shape human behavior and knowledge gaps on Wikipedia and OpenStreetMap
  • Stakeholder AI Evaluation · mixed-methods work building tools that let stakeholders audit and evaluate AI in their own contexts

I publish at CHI, CSCW, FAccT, AIED, and CSCL. I'm a member of the Collaborative Computing Group (MadCollab), advised by Dr. Jacob Thebault-Spieker, and I collaborate with Dr. Shamya Karumbaiah in the Responsible AI for Learning (TRAIL) Lab. Before my PhD, I earned an M.S. in Urban Data Science from New York University.

Research interests: human-computer interaction · responsible AI · AI evaluation & auditing · computational social science · peer production

When AI Breaks, Teachers Repair: Pedagogical Repair Work in Situated Classroom Practice
Yaxuan Yin, Shamya Karumbaiah, Yujin Kim, Devansh Saxena
ACM FAccT 2026
What happens when classroom AI breaks down? We document the pedagogical repair work teachers perform to keep AI-supported instruction running, and argue that this often invisible labor should reshape how educational AI is designed and evaluated.

Beyond Access: Contextualizing the Benefits of Broadband through Contributor Dynamics on Wikipedia
Yaxuan Yin, Jacob Thebault-Spieker
ACM CHI 2026
Does broadband access translate into online participation? Linking broadband connectivity to contributor dynamics on Wikipedia, we show that the benefits of access are contextual, shaped by who contributes, and from where.
